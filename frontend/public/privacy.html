Privacy Policy

SOC 5 Outbound · Effective June 29, 2026

Deployment requirement: The application owner must review this policy against the organization’s actual retention, support, and privacy practices before production publication.

This policy explains how SOC 5 Outbound (“the application”) handles information when authorized personnel use the internal truck-request and outbound-operations service.

1. Information we collect

When an FTE signs in with Google, the application receives basic account information supplied through Google OAuth: the user’s name, email address, Google account identifier, profile image if available, and authentication metadata. The application requests only the openid, email, and profile scopes.

The application also processes information generated through its use, including assigned role, employee or OPS identifier, truck requests, assignments, docking activity, remarks, notifications, status changes, timestamps, and audit events. Technical logs may include IP address, browser and device information, request identifiers, error details, and security events.

2. How information is used

Information is used only to:

  • authenticate users and match them to the approved personnel allowlist;
  • enforce role-based access and protect the application;
  • operate truck-request, assignment, docking, notification, and audit workflows;
  • investigate errors, misuse, security incidents, and operational disputes; and
  • meet applicable operational, compliance, and recordkeeping obligations.

Google user data is not used for advertising, profiling, or sale. The application does not access Gmail, Google Drive, Google Calendar, Google Contacts, or other Google content.

3. Sharing and service providers

Information is available only to authorized personnel with a business need and to service providers that operate the application. Supabase provides authentication and hosted data services. The deployment infrastructure and monitoring providers may process limited technical information needed to host, secure, and support the service. Information may also be disclosed when required by law, lawful process, or a legitimate security investigation.

The application does not sell or rent personal information.

4. Storage, security, and retention

Reasonable administrative and technical controls are used, including authenticated sessions, role-based authorization, an employee allowlist, encrypted transport, restricted service credentials, database access controls, and audit logging. No system can guarantee absolute security.

Information is retained for as long as needed for active operations, security, audit, compliance, and legal obligations, then deleted or de-identified according to the organization’s approved retention procedures. Disabling an account prevents application access but may not immediately remove operational records that must remain attributable for audit purposes.

5. User choices and requests

Authorized users may ask the SOC 5 Outbound administrator or their manager to review or correct their profile information, disable access, or submit an applicable privacy request. Some operational and audit records may need to be retained where required for security, compliance, or legitimate business purposes.

Users can revoke the application’s Google authorization from their Google Account security settings. Revocation ends use of that Google authorization but does not automatically delete records already required for operational or audit purposes.

6. Google API data

The application’s use and transfer of information received from Google APIs adheres to the Google API Services User Data Policy, including its Limited Use requirements. Google information is used only for account authentication, identity matching, access control, and security.

7. Changes to this policy

This policy may be updated when the application, legal requirements, or data practices change. The effective date above will be revised, and material changes will be communicated through appropriate internal channels.

8. Contact

For privacy questions or requests, contact the SOC 5 Outbound application owner through the organization’s approved internal IT or data-privacy support channel.
